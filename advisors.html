Meet Our Advisors

Please click on any advisor above to read their detailed profile.

Justice Rajiv Sharma

Justice Rajiv Sharma brings over 30 years of judicial expertise to our board. As a former High Court Judge known for his landmark environmental judgments, he has pioneered legal reforms across multiple states. His expertise in constitutional law and human rights has shaped national policy. Justice Sharma leads our Ethics Committee and provides strategic guidance on our moot court competitions. His connections with judicial academies across India help our students access premier training opportunities. Justice Sharma is particularly passionate about mentoring first-generation lawyers and creating pathways for underprivileged students to excel in international advocacy competitions.

Dr. Meenakshi Arora

Dr. Meenakshi Arora is a distinguished Senior Advocate practicing at the Supreme Court of India with expertise in constitutional litigation and international arbitration. With numerous landmark cases to her credit, she brings invaluable practical insights to our coaching programs. Dr. Arora has represented India at the ICJ and serves on multiple international legal committees. She holds a doctorate in International Law from Oxford University and has authored three acclaimed books on cross-border litigation. Through her extensive network across Asia and Europe, she helps our students secure prestigious internships and competition opportunities. Dr. Arora leads our International Moot Court Training cell.

Prof. Arjun Kapoor

Prof. Arjun Kapoor is a globally recognized authority on international law and diplomatic negotiations. With previous experience at the United Nations and the ICJ, he brings unparalleled expertise in public international law moots. Prof. Kapoor has coached winning teams at Jessup, Vis, and FDI moot competitions for over fifteen years. His innovative teaching methodologies have transformed advocacy training across South Asia. He maintains close connections with leading international law firms and helps our students navigate complex global competitions. Prof. Kapoor's research on comparative advocacy techniques has been adopted by law schools worldwide. He currently spearheads our Global Advocacy Training Program.

Adv. Priya Malhotra

Advocate Priya Malhotra specializes in constitutional litigation with particular focus on civil liberties and fundamental rights. A National Law School gold medalist, she represents marginalized communities in high-profile constitutional challenges. Adv. Malhotra was herself a distinguished mooter, having won the Best Speaker award at Jessup and Nelson Mandela competitions. Her practical approach to constitutional interpretation has influenced our training methodology for domestic moot competitions. She conducts specialized workshops on written advocacy and memorial preparation. Adv. Malhotra's network of constitutional lawyers provides our students with exceptional mentorship opportunities. She leads our Constitutional Law Research Division.

Dr. Vikram Singh

Dr. Vikram Singh serves as the Dean of one of India's premier National Law Universities, bringing academic excellence and administrative experience to our board. His research on legal education reforms has influenced curriculum development across Indian law schools. Dr. Singh has pioneered specialized courses on advocacy, negotiation, and client counseling at the university level. He maintains strong connections with law schools across five continents, facilitating exchange programs for our students. Dr. Singh's background in both common law and civil law jurisdictions helps our teams adapt to varied competition formats. He oversees our Academic Standards Committee and scholarship programs.

Justice Leela Krishnan

Justice Leela Krishnan's illustrious career spans four decades, culminating in her tenure as a Supreme Court Justice. Known for her progressive judgments in gender justice and commercial law, she brings judicial perspective to our advocacy training. Justice Krishnan has served on numerous international tribunals and arbitration panels. Her experience as a judicial evaluator in moot competitions for twenty years provides invaluable insights into effective advocacy techniques. Justice Krishnan mentors our women advocates through specialized programs and scholarships. She has authored definitive works on judicial interpretation that guide our memorial preparation strategy. Justice Krishnan leads our Judicial Engagement Initiative.
